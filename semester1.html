PROOF ON MEETING GOALS

My first goal was to start planning my time more. Above, you can find photos of my bullet journal (left) that I used to use monthly for planning. However, since December I have lacked using the bullet journal and in January I moved to use a shared digital calendar with my friend to motivate each other. This has become really helpful since we are not only keeping our school organised, but also keep each other motivated. Another good implementation of this goal was my process of writing my academic essay: I first brainstormed on ideas to write about, selected the one that was most, interesting to me, made it more detailed, did research on the topic, wrote down every interesting point I found, created a base for my essay, wrote out the points, read through it and added more ideas, and did final grammar checks and got feedback from peers. This process was very useful and didn’t feel overwhelming since I planned my work over a period of time.

On my goal regarding creativity, I don’t have much proof since I didn’t have time, motivation, or inspiration to work on my creativity this semester. However, I always write down small ideas that could be little pieces of poetry, songs, or ideas for podcasts and short films on my phone. My short film note has been growing since the spring of 2020 and I like to write poems whenever I feel inspired. The poetry notes have been up since summer and have a collection of poems inside of them.
